How Social Media Rewires the Teenage Brain (And Why We Keep Scrolling Anyway)

Social media has become an influential part of all of our lives, especially those of us who are teenagers. Once marketed as a way of connecting with old friends, social media platforms like TikTok and Instagram have become black holes of time and energy. It is not uncommon to have an average screentime of several hours per day on just those two apps. Over the course of a couple years, people amass thousands of hours doom scrolling without a thought in mind. This doesn't even mention the way social media destroys attention span or the suspicious tracking of our every move.
So how do these companies keep us coming back? It would seem as though people wouldn't want to waste a second of their lives, let alone tens of thousands of hours rotting in bed, eyes glued to their screens. How is it that whenever deleted, these apps keep coming back to waste more time? The answer is pretty simple...
